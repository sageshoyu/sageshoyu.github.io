UPDATE: I'm starting grad school this month, and so this website is currently under reconstruction. Welcome to my website! I spend most of my time thinking about how to apply differential geometry, dynamical systems, machine learning, and control theory to problems in robotic manipulation.

I'm an undergraduate senior pursuing a joint degree in Mathematics and Computer Science at Brown University. I'm also a researcher in the Intelligent Robot Lab at Brown advised by Prof. George Konidaris and Prof. Stefanie Tellex.

This past summer, I was also fortunate to be a research intern at Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories under Dr. Daniel Nikovski. My work there was done in collaboration with Dr. Devesh Jha and Dr. Diego Romeres (stay tuned for some exciting work coming out soon!).

I am also an observant Jew (if it's a Jewish holiday, I'll get back to you as soon as I can when the holiday is over).

Riemannian motion policies for safe impedance control in contact-rich manipulation
Seiji Shaw, Ben Abbatematteo, George Konidaris
International Conference on Robotics and Automation, 2022

We propose a new policy class, RMP-VICES, that leverages the Riemannian Motion Policy (RMP) motion-generation framework to synthesize a reinforcement-learning based controller in variable impedance end-effector space (VICES) with safe behavior for robust contact-rich manipulation. [arXiv]
